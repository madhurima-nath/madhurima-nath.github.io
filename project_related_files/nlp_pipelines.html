NLP Pipelines

Three NLP engagements applying text classification, sentiment analysis, and topic modelling to domain-specific document collections. Each addressed a different operational problem — product taxonomy harmonisation, safety insight extraction, and legal document review — using model architectures suited to the data volume and task.

Global Procurement — Consumer Goods

One of the world's largest consumer goods companies managed a $34B spend portfolio spread across multiple ERP systems with no common taxonomy for products, spend categories, or supplier data. Consolidating this data required weeks of manual effort; even small efficiency improvements would translate to millions of dollars in savings.

The engagement designed and built an end-to-end solution on Azure Databricks: metadata-driven ingestion and processing pipelines, a text classification model using RandomForest to harmonise product taxonomy across systems, and dashboards for key spend metrics. MLflow-integrated pipelines and a CI/CD framework handled deployment. The solution delivered improved visibility and consistency across global operations, and accelerated the client's data foundation roadmap by approximately one year.

Safety Analysis — Energy Utility

A large Midwest energy utility needed to extract safety-related themes from incident reports to support policy decisions by its executive safety committee. The volume and variety of reports made manual review impractical.

NLP pipelines were built on Azure Databricks using sentiment analysis and topic modelling to surface recurring themes and patterns across incident data. The output fed into automated weekly reports and refreshed dashboards delivered directly to the safety committee, enabling the team to identify trends, refine policies, and track the effect of interventions on incident frequency and severity.

Document Review — Legal Services

A legal services client needed to reduce the volume of manual document review in e-discovery workflows, where large collections of documents must be classified for relevance to a case.

A document classification system was built using Word2Vec embeddings and SVM classifiers on AWS SageMaker. An automated retraining pipeline enabled incremental model updates as new labelled data became available, keeping the classifier current without full retraining. The system reduced manual review hours and the associated costs.
