Dissertation & Patent

Application of Network Reliability to Analyze Diffusive Processes on Graph Dynamical Systems — Ph.D. Dissertation, Virginia Tech, 2019.

Learn more about the dissertation research

Moore-Shannon network reliability serves as a global statistic to explore the behavior of diffusive processes on networks. It depends on both network topology and the dynamics of the process, giving the probability that the system reaches a desired configuration. The research demonstrates its utility across three domains: epidemic outbreak modeling, invasive species tracking through international food trade, and partition function estimation for the Ising model.

Epidemic Modeling on Contact Networks

Used network reliability to study how network topology affects epidemic outbreak sensitivity. Applied SIR dynamics on real contact networks and statistical graph models, showing that network reliability captures structural effects beyond traditional measures like degree distribution.

Contagion in Food Trade Networks

Applied network reliability to identify dynamics-induced contagion clusters in international food trade networks constructed from UN Comtrade data. Forecasted the impact of mitigating contagion conditions with approximately 96% precision.

Ising Model & Statistical Physics

Established that the network reliability polynomial is analogous to the Ising partition function. Used Bernstein polynomial interpolation to estimate partition functions from low- and high-temperature expansions, reducing time complexity from O(n2) to O(n).

Academic Research Experience

Post-doctoral Research Assistant, Virginia Tech (2019)

Perturbative Methods for Network Reliability Estimation

Improved the time complexity to estimate Moore-Shannon network reliability on a graph by combining Monte Carlo simulations with weak- and strong-coupling perturbative methods. Extended these techniques to heterogeneous satisfiability problems with event-specific probabilities, introducing novel lower and upper bounds on approximation error for monotonic problems.

Community Detection in International Trade Networks

Implemented statistical approaches for community detection in large-scale weighted directed networks using MSNR-CD (Moore-Shannon Network Reliability – Community Detection). Applied computational methods to analyze international trade network dynamics from FAO and UN Comtrade data to identify crucial communities for preventing global pestilence distribution, revealing geographic contiguity patterns in food trade.

Graduate Research Assistant, Virginia Tech (2014–2018)

Network Reliability Estimation Methods

Developed new approaches to evaluate Moore-Shannon network reliability using Bernstein basis functions, Monte Carlo simulations, and statistical perturbative methods. The reliability polynomial R(x; G, D, P) gives the probability that a graph dynamical system satisfies a desired property under given dynamics. Established an adaptive kernel density estimation method using Bernstein polynomials to find optimal sampling strategies that maximise information gain while minimising estimation error.

Related: [1], [3], [6]

Epidemic Outbreak Modeling on Contact Networks

Simulated real-world epidemic outbreak and intervention scenarios on the National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent to Adult Health (Add Health) dataset using network reliability. Compared SIR dynamics on observed contact networks against four classes of exponential random graph models (ERGMs) to determine whether standard network statistics suffice to characterise epidemic outcomes. The analysis revealed that attack rate depends on a mixture of structural properties beyond degree distribution, clustering, and centrality measures.

Related: [4], [5]

Invasive Species & International Food Trade Network Analysis

Identified vulnerabilities within global food trade networks using network reliability to track contagion risk of invasive species (e.g., Tuta absoluta). Constructed directed weighted networks from UN Comtrade data for tomato, potato, eggplant, and aggregate trade between 2005–2013. Developed the concept of contagion clusters — strongly connected components where trade-driven spread is most likely — and forecasted the impact of mitigating contagion conditions with approximately 96% precision.

Related: [2]

Ising Model Partition Function Estimation

Developed an algorithm using Moore-Shannon network reliability to estimate energy states of an interacting magnetic system modeled on 1D and 2D lattices. By treating spin flips as a diffusive process on a graph dynamical system, established that the reliability polynomial is analogous to the Ising partition function. Used Bernstein polynomial interpolation between low- and high-temperature Taylor expansions to reduce time complexity from O(n2) to O(n).
