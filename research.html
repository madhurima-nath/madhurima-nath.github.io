Academic Research Experience

Post-doctoral Research Assistant, Virginia Tech (2019)

  • Improved the time complexity to estimate Moore-Shannon network reliability on a graph by combining Monte Carlo simulations with weak- and strong-coupling perturbative methods.
  • Implemented statistical approaches for community detection in large-scale weighted directed networks, achieving significant improvements over traditional methods.
  • Applied computational methods to analyze international trade network dynamics from the UN Comtrade database to identify crucial communities for preventing global pestilence distribution.

Graduate Research Assistant, Virginia Tech (2014-2018)

  • Developed new approaches to evaluate Moore-Shannon network reliability using Bernstein basis functions, Monte Carlo simulations, and statistical perturbative methods.
  • Applied Moore-Shannon network reliability to predict final global states of graph dynamical systems, analyzing how interactions between node states and connections determine outcomes in practical applications.
  • Simulated real-world epidemic outbreak and intervention scenarios on the National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent to Adult Health dataset using network reliability, providing policymakers improved estimates.
  • Identified vulnerabilities within global food trade networks and forecasted the impact of mitigating contagion conditions with approximately 96% precision.
  • Developed an algorithm using Moore-Shannon network reliability to estimate energy states of an interacting magnetic system, reducing time complexity from O(n2) to O(n).
