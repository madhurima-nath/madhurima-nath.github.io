I've always been fascinated by how local interactions shape global behaviour, uncovering the mathematical principles behind why things behave as they do. This curiosity drives my research and professional work, where I apply foundational physics concepts to explain complex real-world systems.
My academic journey began with master's research in cold atomic physics, published in Physical Review A, before completing my PhD in an interdisciplinary research environment. Working across disciplines allowed me to apply computational physics and graph theory to traditional physics problems while addressing real-world challenges like epidemic outbreaks.
This experience with population dynamics provided solid foundations in data science and machine learning that I now leverage at Slalom. Here, I design and implement data and machine learning/AI solutions across industries, translating theoretical models into practical business applications. I enjoy working both independently and collaboratively on enterprise-wide technical implementations.
I maintain active connections with scientific communities through peer review and support initiatives bridging academic research with industry applications. This reflects my commitment to knowledge exchange and cross-disciplinary collaboration, always seeking new patterns and expanding my understanding of complex systems.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
