I've always been fascinated by how local interactions shape global behaviour, uncovering the mathematical principles behind why things behave as they do. This curiosity drives my work applying physics concepts to real-world challenges.
My academic journey progressed from master's research in cold atomic physics, to a PhD in an interdisciplinary environment. There, I applied computational physics and graph theory to both traditional physics problems and practical challenges like epidemic outbreaks. This experience with population dynamics provided solid foundations in data science and machine learning (ML).
Currently, at Slalom, I design and implement data and ML/AI solutions across industries, translating statistical models into practical business applications. I enjoy working both independently and collaboratively on enterprise-wide technical implementations.
I maintain active connections with scientific communities through peer review and support initiatives bridging academia with industry. This reflects my commitment to knowledge exchange and cross-disciplinary collaboration.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
