I have always been fascinated by how local interactions shape global behaviour, uncovering the mathematical principles behind why things behave as they do. This curiosity drives my work applying physics concepts to real-world challenges.

My academic and postdoctoral work applied computational physics and graph theory to epidemic outbreaks, international trade networks, and the Ising model — a grounding in complex systems that shaped my approach to data science and machine learning.

At Slalom, I design and implement data and AI/ML solutions across industries, working independently and collaboratively on enterprise-wide technical implementations.

I maintain active connections with scientific communities through peer review and initiatives bridging academia with industry.
