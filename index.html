I have always been fascinated by how local interactions shape global behaviour, uncovering the mathematical principles behind why things behave as they do. This curiosity drives my work applying physics concepts to real-world challenges.

My doctoral and postdoctoral work at Virginia Tech, with Prof. Stephen Eubank, used statistical physics and graph theory to study collective dynamics on complex networks — from how epidemics spread through populations, to vulnerabilities in global food trade, to phase behaviour in magnetic systems. My master's thesis explored quantum many-body physics in cold atomic systems.

At Slalom, I design and implement data and AI/ML solutions across industries, from early-stage prototyping through to production deployment, working independently and collaboratively on enterprise-wide technical implementations.

I stay connected to scientific communities through peer review and outreach.
