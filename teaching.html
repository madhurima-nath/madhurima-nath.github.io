PHYS 2984 Waves & Sound

Builds physical intuition for wave phenomena — from sinusoidal motion and energy transport through superposition and standing waves — connecting to real-world acoustic applications including the Doppler effect and 3D interference.

  • Mechanical waves & sound
  • Transverse & sinusoidal waves
  • Power & intensity
  • Doppler Effect
  • Superposition of waves
  • Standing waves on a string
  • Standing sound waves
  • Interference in 2D & 3D
PHYS 2984 Optics

Develops a unified picture of light as both ray and wave: geometric optics covers reflection, refraction, and image formation; physical optics addresses interference and diffraction, bridging everyday phenomena to optical instrument design.

  • Ray model for light
  • Reflection & refraction
  • Image formation by mirrors
  • Image formation by lenses
  • Wave model of light
  • Interference of light waves
  • Diffraction of light waves
PHYS 2984 Thermal Physics

Connects macroscopic thermodynamics — temperature, heat, and work — to microscopic statistical descriptions of molecular motion and pressure, culminating in the laws of thermodynamics and their application to heat engines and refrigerators.

  • Solids, liquids & gases
  • Temperature & ideal gas law
  • Work, heat & first law of thermodynamics
  • Ideal gas processes
  • Molecular speeds & pressure
  • Heat engines & refrigerators
  • Second law of thermodynamics
